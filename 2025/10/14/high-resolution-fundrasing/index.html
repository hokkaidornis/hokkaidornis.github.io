高分辨率融资

High Resolution Fundraising

高分辨率融资

原文链接: https://paulgraham.com/hiresfund.html

September 2010

The reason startups have been using more convertible notes in angel rounds is that they make deals close faster. By making it easier for startups to give different prices to different investors, they help them break the sort of deadlock that happens when investors all wait to see who else is going to invest.初创公司在天使轮融资中越来越多地使用可转换票据，原因在于这种方式能让交易更快达成。可转换票据便于初创公司为不同投资者提供不同定价，从而帮助它们打破一种僵局 —— 即所有投资者都在观望，等待其他人先决定投资的局面。

By far the biggest influence on investors’ opinions of a startup is the opinion of other investors. There are very, very few who simply decide for themselves. Any startup founder can tell you the most common question they hear from investors is not about the founders or the product, but “who else is investing?”到目前为止，影响投资者对某家初创公司看法的最关键因素，是其他投资者的态度。能完全独立做出投资决策的投资者少之又少。任何初创公司创始人都能告诉你，他们从投资者口中听到的最常见问题，并非关于创始人或产品，而是 “还有谁在投资？”

That tends to produce deadlocks. Raising an old-fashioned fixed-size equity round can take weeks, because all the angels sit around waiting for the others to commit, like competitors in a bicycle sprint who deliberately ride slowly at the start so they can follow whoever breaks first.这种心态很容易导致僵局。通过传统的固定规模股权融资轮次筹集资金可能需要数周时间，因为所有天使投资者都会观望，等待其他人先承诺投资 —— 这就像自行车短道赛中的选手，在比赛开始时故意放慢速度，以便能跟随着第一个加速的人前进。

Convertible notes let startups beat such deadlocks by rewarding investors willing to move first with lower (effective) valuations. Which they deserve because they’re taking more risk. It’s much safer to invest in a startup Ron Conway has already invested in; someone who comes after him should pay a higher price.可转换票据能帮助初创公司打破这种僵局：对于愿意率先投资的投资者，公司会以更低的（实际）估值作为回报。这些投资者理应获得这样的待遇，因为他们承担了更高的风险。投资一家已有罗恩・康威（Ron Conway，知名天使投资人）投资的初创公司，风险会低得多；而在他之后进入的投资者，理应支付更高的价格。

The reason convertible notes allow more flexibility in price is that valuation caps aren’t actual valuations, and notes are cheap and easy to do. So you can do high-resolution fundraising: if you wanted you could have a separate note with a different cap for each investor.可转换票据之所以能在定价上提供更高灵活性，是因为估值上限并非实际估值，而且发行票据的成本低、操作简便。因此，你可以开展 “高分辨率融资”：理论上，你可以为每位投资者单独发行票据，并设定不同的估值上限。

That cap need not simply rise monotonically. A startup could also give better deals to investors they expected to help them most. The point is simply that different investors, whether because of the help they offer or their willingness to commit, have different values for startups, and their terms should reflect that.估值上限不一定非要单调递增。初创公司也可以为那些它们认为能提供最大帮助的投资者，提供更优惠的条件。核心在于：不同投资者对初创公司的价值贡献是不同的（无论是基于他们能提供的帮助，还是他们率先承诺投资的意愿），因此融资条款也应体现这种差异。

Different terms for different investors is clearly the way of the future. Markets always evolve toward higher resolution. You may not need to use convertible notes to do it. With sufficiently lightweight standardized equity terms (and some changes in investors’ and lawyers’ expectations about equity rounds) you might be able to do the same thing with equity instead of debt. Either would be fine with startups, so long as they can easily change their valuation.为不同投资者提供不同条款，显然是未来的发展方向。市场总是朝着 “更高分辨率” 的方向演变。要实现这一点，你未必一定要使用可转换票据。只要股权条款足够简化、标准化（同时投资者和律师对股权融资轮次的预期做出一些调整），你或许可以用股权而非债务工具来实现同样的目标。对初创公司而言，这两种方式都行得通，关键在于它们能轻松调整估值。

Deadlocks weren’t the only problem with fixed-size equity rounds. Another was that startups had to decide in advance how much to raise. I think it’s a mistake for a startup to fix upon a specific number. If investors are easily convinced, the startup should raise more now, and if investors are skeptical, the startup should take a smaller amount and use that to get the company to the point where it’s more convincing.僵局并非固定规模股权融资轮次的唯一问题。另一个问题是，初创公司必须提前确定融资额度。在我看来，初创公司固定一个具体的融资数额是错误的。如果投资者很容易被说服，公司就应该现在多筹集一些资金；如果投资者持怀疑态度，公司就应该少筹集一些资金，并用这笔钱推动公司发展到更具说服力的阶段。

It’s just not reasonable to expect startups to pick an optimal round size in advance, because that depends on the reactions of investors, and those are impossible to predict.期望初创公司提前确定 “最优融资规模” 是不现实的，因为这一规模取决于投资者的反应，而投资者的反应根本无法预测。

Fixed-size, multi-investor angel rounds are such a bad idea for startups that one wonders why things were ever done that way. One possibility is that this custom reflects the way investors like to collude when they can get away with it. But I think the actual explanation is less sinister. I think angels (and their lawyers) organized rounds this way in unthinking imitation of VC series A rounds. In a series A, a fixed-size equity round with a lead makes sense, because there is usually just one big investor, who is unequivocally the lead. Fixed-size series A rounds already are high res. But the more investors you have in a round, the less sense it makes for everyone to get the same price.对初创公司而言，固定规模、多投资者参与的天使轮融资是个糟糕的选择，以至于人们会疑惑：这种方式为何会存在？一种可能性是，这种惯例反映了投资者在条件允许时倾向于串通的心态。但我认为实际原因并没有那么阴暗。我认为，天使投资者（及其律师）是在不假思索地模仿风投的 A 轮融资模式，才会以这种方式组织融资。在 A 轮融资中，由领投方主导的固定规模股权融资是合理的，因为通常只有一个大型投资者，且其领投地位明确。固定规模的 A 轮融资本身就具备 “高分辨率” 的特点。但在一轮融资中，投资者数量越多，让所有人都以相同价格投资的合理性就越低。

The most interesting question here may be what high res fundraising will do to the world of investors. Bolder investors will now get rewarded with lower prices. But more important, in a hits-driven business, is that they’ll be able to get into the deals they want. Whereas the “who else is investing?” type of investors will not only pay higher prices, but may not be able to get into the best deals at all.此处最值得探讨的问题或许是：高分辨率融资会给投资者群体带来什么影响？更大胆的投资者如今将获得更低的投资价格作为回报。但更重要的是，在这个 “爆款驱动” 的行业里，他们将有机会参与到自己心仪的投资项目中。而那些总在问 “还有谁在投资？” 的投资者，不仅要支付更高的价格，甚至可能根本无法参与到最优质的项目中。
