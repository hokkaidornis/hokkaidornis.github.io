我们在创始人身上看重的特质

What We Look for in Founders

我们在创始人身上看重的特质

原文链接: https://paulgraham.com/founders.html

October 2010

(I wrote this for Forbes, who asked me to write something about the qualities we look for in founders. In print they had to cut the last item because they didn’t have room.)（本文为《福布斯》杂志撰写，当时杂志要求我写一篇关于我们在创始人身上看重哪些特质的文章。但由于版面有限，印刷版删减了最后一个特质。）

Determination

坚定执着

This has turned out to be the most important quality in startup founders. We thought when we started Y Combinator that the most important quality would be intelligence. That’s the myth in the Valley. And certainly you don’t want founders to be stupid. But as long as you’re over a certain threshold of intelligence, what matters most is determination. You’re going to hit a lot of obstacles. You can’t be the sort of person who gets demoralized easily.事实证明，这是初创公司创始人最重要的特质。我们创立 Y Combinator（创业孵化器）之初，曾认为最重要的特质是智力。这是硅谷流传的固有认知。当然，我们不希望创始人愚笨，但只要智力达到一定门槛，最重要的就是坚定执着。创业过程中会遇到无数障碍，你绝不能是那种轻易气馁的人。

Bill Clerico and Rich Aberman of WePay are a good example. They’re doing a finance startup, which means endless negotiations with big, bureaucratic companies. When you’re starting a startup that depends on deals with big companies to exist, it often feels like they’re trying to ignore you out of existence. But when Bill Clerico starts calling you, you may as well do what he asks, because he is not going away.WePay 公司的比尔・克莱里科（Bill Clerico）和里奇・阿伯曼（Rich Aberman）就是很好的例子。他们在做一家金融科技初创公司，这意味着要与庞大、官僚的大公司进行无休止的谈判。当你的初创公司依赖与大公司合作才能生存时，常会感觉这些大公司试图通过无视让你自生自灭。但只要比尔・克莱里科开始联系你，你最好按他的要求做，因为他绝不会轻易放弃。

Flexibility

灵活应变

You do not however want the sort of determination implied by phrases like “don’t give up on your dreams.” The world of startups is so unpredictable that you need to be able to modify your dreams on the fly. The best metaphor I’ve found for the combination of determination and flexibility you need is a running back. He’s determined to get downfield, but at any given moment he may need to go sideways or even backwards to get there.但我们不推崇 “不放弃梦想” 这类说法所暗示的那种 “执着”。初创行业的不确定性极高，你需要能随时调整自己的目标。对于 “坚定与灵活并存”，我能想到的最佳比喻是美式橄榄球中的跑卫：他的目标明确 —— 冲向前场，但在过程中，他可能需要随时向侧面甚至向后跑，才能最终达成目标。

The current record holder for flexibility may be Daniel Gross of Greplin. He applied to YC with some bad ecommerce idea. We told him we’d fund him if he did something else. He thought for a second, and said ok. He then went through two more ideas before settling on Greplin. He’d only been working on it for a couple days when he presented to investors at Demo Day, but he got a lot of interest. He always seems to land on his feet.目前，在 “灵活应变” 方面最突出的例子可能是 Greplin 公司的丹尼尔・格罗斯（Daniel Gross）。他最初带着一个糟糕的电子商务想法申请 YC 孵化。我们告诉他，只要换个方向，我们就会投资他。他思考了一秒钟，随即同意了。之后，他又尝试了两个想法，最终确定了 Greplin 的方向。在演示日（Demo Day）向投资者展示时，他只做了这个项目几天，但还是引起了大量关注。他似乎总能在变化中站稳脚跟。

Imagination

想象力

Intelligence does matter a lot of course. It seems like the type that matters most is imagination. It’s not so important to be able to solve predefined problems quickly as to be able to come up with surprising new ideas. In the startup world, most good ideas seem bad initially. If they were obviously good, someone would already be doing them. So you need the kind of intelligence that produces ideas with just the right level of craziness.当然，智力也非常重要，但最重要的智力类型似乎是想象力。相比快速解决预设好的问题，能提出令人惊喜的新想法更为关键。在初创领域，大多数好想法起初看起来都像 “坏主意”。如果一个想法显然是好的，早就有人去做了。因此，你需要的是那种能产生 “疯狂程度恰到好处” 的想法的智力。

Airbnb is that kind of idea. In fact, when we funded Airbnb, we thought it was too crazy. We couldn’t believe large numbers of people would want to stay in other people’s places. We funded them because we liked the founders so much. As soon as we heard they’d been supporting themselves by selling Obama and McCain branded breakfast cereal, they were in. And it turned out the idea was on the right side of crazy after all.Airbnb 就是这样的想法。事实上，我们当初投资 Airbnb 时，曾认为这个想法太疯狂了 —— 我们无法相信会有大量人愿意住在陌生人家里。我们决定投资，主要是因为非常欣赏创始人团队。当我们听说他们曾通过销售印有奥巴马和麦凯恩（美国政客）头像的早餐麦片来维持生计时，就确定要投资他们了。事实证明，这个想法的 “疯狂程度” 最终恰到好处。

Naughtiness

略带 “顽皮”

Though the most successful founders are usually good people, they tend to have a piratical gleam in their eye. They’re not Goody Two-Shoes type good. Morally, they care about getting the big questions right, but not about observing proprieties. That’s why I’d use the word naughty rather than evil. They delight in breaking rules, but not rules that matter. This quality may be redundant though; it may be implied by imagination.最成功的创始人通常都是好人，但他们眼中往往带着一丝 “海盗般的狡黠”。他们不是那种循规蹈矩、完美无缺的 “乖乖牌”。在道德层面，他们在意大是大非的判断，但不拘泥于繁文缛节。这就是为什么我用 “顽皮” 而非 “邪恶” 来形容他们 —— 他们喜欢打破规则，但从不打破重要的规则。不过，这种特质可能是想象力的衍生，并非独立存在。

Sam Altman of Loopt is one of the most successful alumni, so we asked him what question we could put on the Y Combinator application that would help us discover more people like him. He said to ask about a time when they’d hacked something to their advantage—hacked in the sense of beating the system, not breaking into computers. It has become one of the questions we pay most attention to when judging applications.Loopt 公司的山姆・奥特曼（Sam Altman）是 YC 最成功的校友之一。我们曾问他，在 YC 的申请表中加入什么问题，能帮助我们找到更多像他这样的人。他建议我们问：“你曾在什么事情上通过‘破解’获得优势？” 这里的 “破解” 指的是巧妙利用规则、突破系统限制，而非入侵电脑。如今，这个问题已成为我们审核申请时最关注的问题之一。

Friendship

良好的伙伴关系

Empirically it seems to be hard to start a startup with just one founder. Most of the big successes have two or three. And the relationship between the founders has to be strong. They must genuinely like one another, and work well together. Startups do to the relationship between the founders what a dog does to a sock: if it can be pulled apart, it will be.从经验来看，单创始人很难成功创办公司。大多数成功的大公司都有两到三位联合创始人，且创始人之间的关系必须稳固。他们必须真心喜欢彼此，并且能良好合作。初创公司对创始人关系的考验，就像狗对袜子的 “考验”：只要能扯断，就一定会被扯断（意即：若关系本身存在裂痕，创业压力一定会让裂痕扩大）。

Emmett Shear and Justin Kan of Justin.tv are a good example of close friends who work well together. They’ve known each other since second grade. They can practically read one another’s minds. I’m sure they argue, like all founders, but I have never once sensed any unresolved tension between them.Justin.tv（直播平台）的埃米特・希尔（Emmett Shear）和贾斯汀・坎（Justin Kan）就是 “好友兼好搭档” 的典范。他们从二年级就认识，几乎能心领神会。我相信他们也会像所有创始人一样发生争执，但我从未察觉到他们之间有任何未解决的矛盾。
