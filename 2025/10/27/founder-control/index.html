创始人控制权

Founder Control

创始人控制权

原文链接: https://paulgraham.com/control.html

December 2010

Someone we funded is talking to VCs now, and asked me how common it was for a startup’s founders to retain control of the board after a series A round. He said VCs told him this almost never happened.我们投资过的一家公司正在与风投接触，创始人问我，初创公司在 A 轮融资后，创始人保留董事会控制权的情况有多普遍。他表示，风投告诉他这种情况几乎不可能发生。

Ten years ago that was true. In the past, founders rarely kept control of the board through a series A. The traditional series A board consisted of two founders, two VCs, and one independent member. More recently the recipe is often one founder, one VC, and one independent. In either case the founders lose their majority.十年前，情况确实如此。过去，创始人很少能在 A 轮融资后仍掌握董事会控制权。传统的 A 轮董事会结构通常是 2 名创始人、2 名风投代表和 1 名独立董事。近年来，常见的结构变成了 1 名创始人、1 名风投代表和 1 名独立董事。无论哪种结构，创始人都失去了多数席位。

But not always. Mark Zuckerberg kept control of Facebook’s board through the series A and still has it today. Mark Pincus has kept control of Zynga’s too. But are these just outliers? How common is it for founders to keep control after an A round? I’d heard of several cases among the companies we’ve funded, but I wasn’t sure how many there were, so I emailed the ycfounders list.但并非所有情况都是如此。马克・扎克伯格在 Facebook 的 A 轮融资后仍保留了董事会控制权，且至今仍掌握着这一权力。马克・平卡斯也始终掌控着 Zynga 的董事会。但这些只是个例吗？A 轮融资后创始人保留控制权的情况到底有多普遍？我听说过我们投资的公司中有几起这样的案例，但不确定具体数量，于是我给 Y Combinator 创始人列表（ycfounders list）发了封邮件询问。

The replies surprised me. In a dozen companies we’ve funded, the founders still had a majority of the board seats after the series A round.回复结果让我感到意外。在我们投资的 12 家公司中，创始人在 A 轮融资后仍持有董事会的多数席位。

I feel like we’re at a tipping point here. A lot of VCs still act as if founders retaining board control after a series A is unheard-of. A lot of them try to make you feel bad if you even ask — as if you’re a noob or a control freak for wanting such a thing. But the founders I heard from aren’t noobs or control freaks. Or if they are, they are, like Mark Zuckerberg, the kind of noobs and control freaks VCs should be trying to fund more of.我感觉我们正处于一个转折点。许多风投仍认为，创始人在 A 轮融资后保留董事会控制权是闻所未闻的事。如果你主动提出这个需求，很多风投还会让你感到难堪 —— 仿佛你是个新手，或是个控制欲极强的人。但我接触到的这些创始人，既不是新手，也不是控制狂。即便他们被贴上这样的标签，他们也像马克・扎克伯格一样，是风投应该更积极投资的那类 “新手” 和 “控制狂”。

Founders retaining control after a series A is clearly heard-of. And barring financial catastrophe, I think in the coming year it will become the norm.显然，A 轮融资后创始人保留控制权的情况并非绝无仅有。且除非发生财务危机，我认为未来一年，这种情况将成为常态。

Control of a company is a more complicated matter than simply outvoting other parties in board meetings. Investors usually get vetos over certain big decisions, like selling the company, regardless of how many board seats they have. And board votes are rarely split. Matters are decided in the discussion preceding the vote, not in the vote itself, which is usually unanimous. But if opinion is divided in such discussions, the side that knows it would lose in a vote will tend to be less insistent. That’s what board control means in practice. You don’t simply get to do whatever you want; the board still has to act in the interest of the shareholders; but if you have a majority of board seats, then your opinion about what’s in the interest of the shareholders will tend to prevail.掌控一家公司，远不止是在董事会会议中通过票数压制其他方那么简单。无论持有多少董事会席位，投资者通常对某些重大决策拥有否决权，比如出售公司。而且董事会投票很少出现分歧，事项往往在投票前的讨论阶段就已确定，而非通过投票本身 —— 投票结果通常是一致通过。但如果讨论中出现意见分歧，知道自己会在投票中落败的一方，往往不会过于坚持自己的观点。这就是董事会控制权在实际中的意义：你不能随心所欲地行事，董事会仍需为股东利益着想；但如果你持有董事会多数席位，你对 “何为股东利益” 的判断，往往会成为最终决策依据。

So while board control is not total control, it’s not imaginary either. There’s inevitably a difference in how things feel within the company. Which means if it becomes the norm for founders to retain board control after a series A, that will change the way things feel in the whole startup world.因此，尽管董事会控制权并非绝对控制权，但也绝非虚无缥缈。它必然会让公司内部的运作氛围产生差异。这意味着，如果 A 轮融资后创始人保留董事会控制权成为常态，整个初创企业领域的生态氛围都将随之改变。

The switch to the new norm may be surprisingly fast, because the startups that can retain control tend to be the best ones. They’re the ones that set the trends, both for other startups and for VCs.向这一新常态的转变可能会快得惊人，因为能够保留控制权的初创公司，往往是最优秀的那一批。它们既是其他初创公司的风向标，也引导着风投的行为趋势。

A lot of the reason VCs are harsh when negotiating with startups is that they’re embarrassed to go back to their partners looking like they got beaten. When they sign a termsheet, they want to be able to brag about the good terms they got. A lot of them don’t care that much personally about whether founders keep board control. They just don’t want to seem like they had to make concessions. Which means if letting the founders keep control stops being perceived as a concession, it will rapidly become much more common.风投在与初创公司谈判时态度强硬，很大程度上是因为他们不想在回到自己的合伙人面前时，显得像是 “输了谈判”。签署投资条款清单时，他们希望能吹嘘自己拿到了优厚的条件。很多风投个人其实并不在意创始人是否保留董事会控制权，他们只是不想让人觉得自己做出了让步。这意味着，一旦 “让创始人保留控制权” 不再被视为一种让步，这种做法就会迅速变得普遍。

Like a lot of changes that have been forced on VCs, this change won’t turn out to be as big a problem as they might think. VCs will still be able to convince; they just won’t be able to compel. And the startups where they have to resort to compulsion are not the ones that matter anyway. VCs make most of their money from a few big hits, and those aren’t them.就像许多强加给风投的变革一样，这种变化最终不会像他们担心的那样成为大问题。风投依然可以通过说服来影响决策，只是无法再强制要求。而且，那些需要风投通过强制手段才能合作的初创公司，原本也不是核心标的。风投的大部分收益来自少数几个 “爆款” 项目，而这类项目绝不是需要靠强制手段才能推进的。

Knowing that founders will keep control of the board may even help VCs pick better. If they know they can’t fire the founders, they’ll have to choose founders they can trust. And that’s who they should have been choosing all along.明确创始人将保留董事会控制权，甚至可能帮助风投做出更优质的投资选择。如果他们知道自己无法解雇创始人，就必须选择那些值得信任的创始人。而这类创始人，本就应该是他们一直以来的投资对象。
