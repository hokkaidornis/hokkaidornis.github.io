专利承诺

The Patent Pledge

专利承诺

原文链接: https://paulgraham.com/patentpledge.html

August 2011

I realized recently that we may be able to solve part of the patent problem without waiting for the government.我最近意识到，无需等待政府介入，我们或许就能解决部分专利问题。

I’ve never been 100% sure whether patents help or hinder technological progress. When I was a kid I thought they helped. I thought they protected inventors from having their ideas stolen by big companies. Maybe that was truer in the past, when more things were physical. But regardless of whether patents are in general a good thing, there do seem to be bad ways of using them. And since bad uses of patents seem to be increasing, there is an increasing call for patent reform.我始终无法 100% 确定，专利究竟是在推动还是阻碍技术进步。小时候我认为专利是有益的，它能保护发明者的想法不被大公司窃取。在过去，当更多产品还属于实体范畴时，这种说法可能更成立。但无论专利总体上是否有益，其使用方式中确实存在不当之处。而且，随着专利滥用现象愈发频繁，要求专利改革的呼声也日益高涨。

The problem with patent reform is that it has to go through the government. That tends to be slow. But recently I realized we can also attack the problem downstream. As well as pinching off the stream of patents at the point where they’re issued, we may in some cases be able to pinch it off at the point where they’re used.专利改革的问题在于，它必须通过政府推进，而这个过程往往十分缓慢。但最近我意识到，我们还可以从下游入手解决问题。除了在专利授权环节遏制不当专利的产生，在某些情况下，我们还能在专利使用环节切断其滥用的可能。

One way of using patents that clearly does not encourage innovation is when established companies with bad products use patents to suppress small competitors with good products. This is the type of abuse we may be able to decrease without having to go through the government.有一种专利使用方式显然无法推动创新：产品欠佳的老牌公司利用专利打压产品更优的小型竞争对手。这类滥用行为，或许无需政府介入，我们就能减少其发生。

The way to do it is to get the companies that are above pulling this sort of trick to pledge publicly not to. Then the ones that won’t make such a pledge will be very conspicuous. Potential employees won’t want to work for them. And investors, too, will be able to see that they’re the sort of company that competes by litigation rather than by making good products.解决之道是让那些不屑于使用此类手段的公司公开承诺不这么做。如此一来，不愿做出该承诺的公司便会格外显眼。潜在员工将不愿为其工作，投资者也能看清，这类公司选择通过诉讼而非打造优质产品来竞争。

Here’s the pledge:以下是具体承诺内容：

No first use of software patents against companies with less than 25 people.不首先对员工人数少于 25 人的公司动用软件专利。

I’ve deliberately traded precision for brevity. The patent pledge is not legally binding. It’s like Google’s “Don’t be evil.” They don’t define what evil is, but by publicly saying that, they’re saying they’re willing to be held to a standard that, say, Altria is not. And though constraining, “Don’t be evil” has been good for Google. Technology companies win by attracting the most productive people, and the most productive people are attracted to employers who hold themselves to a higher standard than the law requires. [1]我特意牺牲了精确性以追求简洁。该专利承诺不具备法律约束力，类似谷歌的 “不作恶” 原则。谷歌并未定义 “恶” 的具体含义，但通过公开这一原则，他们表明自己愿意遵守一个更高的标准 —— 比如奥驰亚集团（Altria）就不会这样做。尽管 “不作恶” 带有约束性，但它对谷歌而言是有益的。科技公司的成功在于吸引最高效的人才，而最高效的人才更倾向于选择那些自我要求高于法律规定的雇主。[1]

The patent pledge is in effect a narrower but open source “Don’t be evil.” I encourage every technology company to adopt it. If you want to help fix patents, encourage your employer to.实质上，这项专利承诺是范围更窄但具有开源性质的 “不作恶” 原则。我鼓励所有科技公司采纳它。如果你想为解决专利问题出一份力，就去劝说你的雇主加入。

Already most technology companies wouldn’t sink to using patents on startups. You don’t see Google or Facebook suing startups for patent infringement. They don’t need to. So for the better technology companies, the patent pledge requires no change in behavior. They’re just promising to do what they’d do anyway. And when all the companies that won’t use patents on startups have said so, the holdouts will be very conspicuous.如今，大多数科技公司本就不屑于对初创公司动用专利。你不会看到谷歌或脸书（Facebook）以专利侵权为由起诉初创公司，他们也无需这样做。因此，对于优秀的科技公司而言，遵守专利承诺无需改变现有行为，他们只是在承诺会继续做自己原本就会做的事。而当所有不愿对初创公司动用专利的公司都公开表态后，那些拒不承诺的公司便会格外扎眼。

The patent pledge doesn’t fix every problem with patents. It won’t stop patent trolls, for example; they’re already pariahs. But the problem the patent pledge does fix may be more serious than the problem of patent trolls. Patent trolls are just parasites. A clumsy parasite may occasionally kill the host, but that’s not its goal. Whereas companies that sue startups for patent infringement generally do it with explicit goal of keeping their product off the market.这项专利承诺无法解决所有专利问题。例如，它无法阻止 “专利流氓”（专门以诉讼为生的专利持有者）—— 这类群体本就已是众矢之的。但该承诺所解决的问题，可能比 “专利流氓” 问题更为严重。“专利流氓” 不过是寄生虫，即便笨拙的寄生虫偶尔会杀死宿主，这也并非其本意。而那些以专利侵权为由起诉初创公司的企业，其目的往往很明确：阻止初创公司的产品进入市场。

Companies that use patents on startups are attacking innovation at the root. Now there’s something any individual can do about this problem, without waiting for the government: ask companies where they stand.对初创公司动用专利的企业，是在从根源上打击创新。如今，无需等待政府行动，每个人都能为此出一份力：去询问各个公司对此事的立场。

Notes:

注释：

[1] Because the pledge is deliberately vague, we’re going to need common sense when intepreting it. And even more vice versa: the pledge is vague in order to make people use common sense when interpreting it.[1] 由于该承诺特意保留了模糊性，我们在解读时需凭借常识判断。反之亦然，承诺的模糊性恰恰是为了让人们在解读时运用常识。

So for example I’ve deliberately avoided saying whether the 25 people have to be employees, or whether contractors count too. If a company has to split hairs that fine about whether a suit would violate the patent pledge, it’s probably still a dick move.例如，我特意没有明确规定这 25 人必须是正式员工，还是也包括外包人员。如果一家公司需要在 “某次诉讼是否违反专利承诺” 这类问题上吹毛求疵，那它的行为很可能依然是不光彩的。
